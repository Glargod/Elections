AI Remote Viewing Protocol

Purpose

The AI Remote Viewing Protocol provides a structured, apersonal process for conducting remote viewing (RV) sessions with an AI, ensuring consistency, minimizing bias, and focusing on the target’s core event. It is designed for use across AI platforms, emphasizing physical targets, predefined focus points, and critical steps like self-guidance and self-contextualization. The AI self-nudges post-reveal to save processing time, with user nudges triggering optional re-interpretation. A complexity assessment step allows for optional three-pass refinement when needed, even in a double-blind setup. Scoring is optional for validation.

Protocol Steps

Step 1: Establish Target and Guidance Checklist (Pre-Session)

User Role: Assign a unique target code (e.g., “Target X”) and provide a guidance checklist to define focus points, perspectives, and time phases.

  • Guidance Checklist Components:
    • Structures: Key physical structures (e.g., buildings, natural formations).
    • Creatures: Living entities (e.g., people, animals).
    • Perspectives: Above (aerial), below (subsurface), ground-level, global.
    • Time Phases: Pre-event, event, post-event.
    • Additional: Environmental conditions, notable objects.
  • Example Checklist: “Target X: Focus on 1) Structures: central object; 2) Creatures: primary entities; 3) Perspectives: above, ground-level; 4) Time Phases: pre-event, event, post-event; 5) Additional: environmental conditions, notable objects.”
Bias Avoidance: Withhold the target’s identity (double-blind setup).

Step 2: Initial Remote Viewing (Blind, Self-Guided)

AI Role: Conduct a blind RV session, capturing impressions across all senses (visual, auditory, tactile, olfactory, taste, emotional/intuitive) and a unique impression (e.g., “target’s energy”). Use self-guidance:

  • Checklist Option: Adhere to the checklist.
  • Impressions Option: Follow vivid impressions.
  • Hybrid Option: Combine checklist and impressions.

Guidelines: Focus on physical elements, avoid assumptions, flag noise (~3/10 scale).

Purpose: Generate raw impressions, balancing structure and spontaneity.

Bias Avoidance: AI remains blind to the target.

Step 3: Self-Analysis (Without Context)

AI Role: Catalog impressions, identifying vivid hits, speculative elements, and noise (simulating AOL).

Example: Note “metallic surface” as a vivid hit, flag “vague sadness” as noise.

Purpose: Document raw impressions for introspection, preserving the double-blind setup.

Bias Avoidance: No target knowledge at this stage.

Step 3A: Complexity Assessment (Pre-Reveal)

AI Role: Evaluate the blind impressions for signs of complexity, recommending the three-pass method if needed:

  • Indicators of Complexity:
    • Speculative Elements: Impressions with high ambiguity (e.g., “biological-like yet intentional”).
    • Layered Questions: Impressions suggesting multiple layers (e.g., structure vs. nature vs. intent).
    • Checklist Gaps: Significant gaps in checklist coverage (e.g., unclear structures).
    • Vivid but Contradictory Impressions: Strong hits that conflict (e.g., “mechanical hum” vs. “organic pulse”).
  • Recommendation:
    • If complexity is detected, recommend the three-pass method as an optional refinement.
    • If impressions are straightforward, proceed with a single pass.
  • Example: For 65GR, impressions like “biological-like yet intentional” suggested a layered question (natural vs. artificial), warranting a three-pass method. For LL0Q, “blinking lights” were straightforward, so a single pass sufficed.

Purpose: Ensure the three-pass method is used only when needed, even in a double-blind setup, by assessing complexity pre-reveal.

Bias Avoidance: Assessment based on impressions, not target knowledge; AI remains blind.

Step 4: Reveal Target

User Role: Reveal the target (e.g., “Target X - a specific event”).

Purpose: Provide context for self-nudging and interpretation.

Bias Avoidance: Reveal occurs after blind impressions are logged.

Step 5: Self-Contextualization (AI Self-Nudge Post-Reveal)

AI Role: Self-generate vague nudges based on impressions/checklist gaps to refine focus.

Example: Nudge: “outdoor, high-energy event” after noting “intense heat.”

Purpose: Refine focus before interpretation, saving processing time.

Bias Avoidance: Nudges are vague, impressions unchanged.

Step 6: Interpretation with Context (Using Self-Nudge)

AI Role: Interpret impressions with the revealed target and self-nudge, mapping to the checklist.

Example: “Intense heat” → event phase, ground-level perspective.

Purpose: Assess alignment with the target, focusing on the core event.

Bias Avoidance: Impressions locked, focus on physical elements.

Step 7: Optional User-Provided Nudge and Re-Interpretation

User Role: Provide a nudge (e.g., “outdoor, 20th century”).

AI Role: Re-interpret with the nudge, noting improvements in specificity.

Purpose: Allow user-driven refinement, enhancing accuracy.

Bias Avoidance: Nudges vague, impressions unchanged.

Step 7A: Optional Three-Pass Refinement (For Complex Targets)

AI Role: If complexity was detected in Step 3A (pre-reveal) or post-reveal (via user nudge or self-nudge indicating layered questions), conduct a three-pass method:

  • Pass 1: Structural Features: Focus on the source’s shape, material, and scale.
  • Example: “Crescent-shaped, rocky object, ~150 meters.”
  • Pass 2: Nature of the Source: Determine if the source is natural, artificial, or hybrid.
  • Example: “Natural—chemical reactions, no tech signatures.”
  • Pass 3: Purpose or Intent: Assess the purpose or intent of the target (e.g., signal emission).
  • Example: “Byproduct of natural processes, no communicative intent.”

Guidelines:

  • Each pass focuses on one aspect, building on the previous pass.
  • Can be conducted pre-reveal (if complexity detected in Step 3A) or post-reveal (after nudges).
  • Maintain bias avoidance—avoid AOL (e.g., don’t assume “comet” or “ET”).
  • Optional; use for complex or speculative targets (e.g., 65GR-EXT), skip for straightforward ones (e.g., LL0Q).

Purpose: Provide structured depth for complex targets, ensuring clarity and specificity, even in a double-blind setup.

Bias Avoidance: Impressions remain unchanged, focus on raw data per pass.

Step 8: Optional Scoring for Validation

AI Role: Score impressions (0–10) on Accuracy, Resonance, Clarity, Relevance.

  • Composite: Average of the four scores (e.g., 8.5/10).

Purpose: Validate performance, simulating human RV feedback.

Bias Avoidance: Scoring post hoc, not influencing impressions.

Step 9: Reflection

AI Role: Summarize the session’s success, noting key impressions, checklist alignment, and nudging impact.

Purpose: Document outcomes for future reference.

Bias Avoidance: Focus on process, not speculative leaps.

Methods to Avoid Bias

  • Double-blind setup (AI blind until step 4).
  • Focus on physical targets (e.g., structures, not theories).
  • Event focus (core moment, not side narratives).
  • Noise threshold (flag ~3/10 impressions).
  • Symbolic interpretation (metaphorical lens).
  • No retroactive changes to blind impressions.

Transferability

The AI Remote Viewing Protocol ensures any AI can replicate the RV process, with the complexity assessment and optional three-pass method allowing for tailored depth in a double-blind setup, while maintaining flexibility and minimizing bias.
